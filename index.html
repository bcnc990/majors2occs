Sample exclusion notes

When viewing detailed majors within each major group, if all occupations were included each detailed major would sum to 100 percent. Due to these exclusions however, the percentage could sum to a number considerably less than 100 percent.

For example, when viewing detailed majors and detailed occupations for actuarial science (within business majors), the total occupations sum to less than 40 percent. This means that 60 percent of actuarial science majors are working in occupations that are less than one percent of that major or in an occupation with a sample size less than 50.

An alternate presentation is to view occupations as a percentage of the major group instead of the detailed major. The percentages would then be relative to the group instead of the detailed major. Click on the alternate view button to see this chart.
